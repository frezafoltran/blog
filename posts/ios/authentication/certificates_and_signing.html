Why sign the iOS app?

The app needs to be signed for security reasons. In the case of iOS apps, üçè requires that apps are signed and authenticated prior to being released in the App Store or installed in iOS devices.

Create an Apple Developer account

If you don't have one, you'll need to create an Apple developer account. You can simply follow the steps here to get one.

Generate a Certificate

Once you have the developer account, go to your Certificates page: developer.apple.com/account/resources/certificates/list If you are still developing the app, you will need to create a Development Certificate. If you are ready to publish your app in the app store, create a Distribution Certificate. Follow the steps on Apple to generate a CSR (Certificate Signing Request) locally and upload it to download the Certificate.

Generate an Identifier

Now you need to register an identifier to identify your app to Apple. In this step, if desired, you can also create a wildcard id that would work with any app.
